GALLERY OF PLANETS

MERCURY

Mercury is the only planet that has a temperature range of over 600 degrees Celsius between day and night due to its thin atmosphere.

VENUS

Venus has the brightest natural object in the sky after the Sun and the Moon. It's often referred to as the "Evening Star" or "Morning Star" because it can be easily seen at dawn or dusk!

EARTH

Earth’s gravity pulls you down, but if you could bounce high enough,
you'd only be weightless for a brief moment
— like a cosmic trampoline!

MARS

Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.
This shield volcano is about 22 km high, nearly 3 times the height of Mount Everest!

JUPITER

Jupiter has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for over 350 years.
It's so large that 3 Earths could fit inside it!

SATURN

Saturn emits radio waves that sound like a strange symphony of squeaks and whistles.
It's like the planet is trying to communicate through music!

URANUS

Uranus is mostly made of ice and gas, so it's basically a giant snowball floating in space and it spins on its side, it's like the planet just can't decide which way is up!

NEPTUNE

Neptune's striking blue color comes from methane in its atmosphere, making it look like the universe's biggest swimming pool!

PLUTO

Pluto was considered the ninth planet until 2006, when it was reclassified as a "dwarf planet."
